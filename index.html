These are my favourite albums

Station To Station
by David Bowie

Taking the detached plastic soul of Young Americans to an elegant, robotic extreme, Station to Station is a transitional album that creates its own distinctive style.


  • Pop/Rock
  • January 1976
  • Duration: 38:21
POP-ROCK
Unknown Pleasures
by Joy Division

Unknown Pleasures has received sustained critical acclaim as an influential post-punk album, and has been named as one of the best albums of all time by publications such as NME, AllMusic, Select, Rolling Stone, and Spin.

  • Post-punk
  • April 1979
  • Duration: 39:28
POST-PUNK
Synchronicty
by The Police

Synchronicity reached number one on both the UK Albums Chart and the US Billboard 200, and sold over eight million copies in the US. The album was widely acclaimed by critics.


  • New wave / Post-punk
  • June 1983
  • Duration: 39:42
NEW-WAVE
So
by Peter Gabriel

Often considered his best and most accessible album, So was an immediate commercial success and transformed Gabriel from a cult artist into a mainstream star, becoming his best-selling solo release.

  • Art Rock
  • May 1986
  • Duration: 41:56
ART ROCK
Let Love In
by Nick Cave

Let Love In is the eighth studio album by Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds, released on 18 April 1994 on Mute Records. As of May 2015 it was certified silver by British Phonographic Industry for 60,000 sold units in UK. As of January 1996 the album has sold 50,000 copies in United States.


  • Post-punk
  • April 1994
  • Duration: 48:18
POST-PUNK
Autobahn
by Kraftwerk

Autobahn is the fourth studio album by German electronic band Kraftwerk, released in November 1974. It was the band's first album to fully embrace the repetitive electronic sound they would become known for (although organic instruments still remained part of their sonic palette) and was inspired by the titular German motorway system.

  • Electronic Pop
  • November 1974
  • Duration: 42:26
ELECTRONIC

And here's my hall of shame

Evis' Greatest Shit
by Elvis Presley

The disc was assembled by a bootlegger known simply as "Richard", who thought some fans were overenthusiastic to the point of deification, and wanted to show that Presley, like most other artists, could not produce exclusively critically acclaimed work throughout his lengthy career.

  • Pop
  • July 1982
  • Duration: too long
POP
Playing With Fire
by Kevin Federline

The only album recorded by Kevin Federline, ex-husband of Britney Spears, Playing with Fire is review aggregator Metacritic's lowest-scoring album with a rating of 15. It was also a commercial failure, with first-week sales of only 6,000 in the U.S.


  • Pop Rap
  • October 2006
  • Duration: 49:36
POP-RAP
Lulu
by Lou Reed & Metallica

Pitchfork Media's Stuart Berman awarded the album a score of 1/10 and wrote that Lulu disappoints even in its "worst of all time" status. "For all the hilarity that ought to ensue here, Lulu is a frustratingly noble failure."


  • Avant-garde metal
  • October 2011
  • Duration: 87:05
AVANTGARDE

A special mention

Sting is one of the very few artists capable of producing extremely complex compositions and arrangements and still make them commercially successful.

He is also an accomplished wordsmith. I wrote this article on Medium about his song All This Time.

More than anything, though, he is the reason I became a musician.
